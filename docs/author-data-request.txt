Dear Dr. Shu, Dr. Yu, and Professor Mulvey,

I am working on an independent replication of your paper, "Downside Risk
Reduction Using Regime-Switching Signals: A Statistical Jump Model Approach."
My immediate goal is to reproduce the fixed Jump Model and HMM results before
testing any model extension.

I implemented the published daily protocol for the S&P 500, DAX, and Nikkei:
trailing 3,000-day windows, semiannual Jump Model refits, daily HMM refits with
10 deterministic initializations, monthly hyperparameter selection using an
eight-year online validation period, a two-day execution delay, and 10 basis
points per one-way trade.

I do not have access to the Bloomberg total-return series or the Global
Financial Data three-month Treasury-bill series used in the paper. I therefore
ran a clearly labeled proxy replication using free public data. The run passes
all internal protocol and artifact checks, but it does not reproduce the
paper's directional ordering across all three markets. I am treating this as a
proxy non-replication, not as evidence against the paper, because important
data definitions and several implementation details remain uncertain.

Could you please share any of the following information that is available?

1. The exact Bloomberg tickers and fields for the three equity total-return
   series, including whether each series is gross or net of withholding tax.
2. The exact Global Financial Data identifiers for the local three-month
   Treasury-bill series.
3. The convention used to convert quoted bill yields into daily cash returns,
   including day count, compounding, lag, and missing-value treatment.
4. The complete Jump Model lambda grid and HMM median-filter grid used for the
   reported tables.
5. Tie-breaking, initialization, convergence, state-labeling, and missing-data
   conventions not fully specified in the paper.
6. If possible, code, processed return series, or checksums that would let an
   independent researcher verify data parity.

I would be glad to share my frozen configuration, source audit, and verified
proxy report. I will report positive, negative, or null replication outcomes
and will distinguish exact replication from proxy evidence.

Thank you for your time and for making the research available.
