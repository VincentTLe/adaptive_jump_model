Research brief · 21 July 2026

Can a Jump Model beat HMM and Buy & Hold?

We rebuilt Shu et al.'s market-or-cash idea on a past-only public proxy sample, then developed evidence-adaptive switch penalties. The target is simple: higher net Sharpe than both benchmarks on the same dates.

Answer so far: only Germany passes. At least one tested JM variant beats both benchmarks only in Germany. The best observed variant still loses to HMM in the US and to buy-and-hold in Japan.

1. What is the paper's idea?

Market behavior appears to persist in favorable and unfavorable periods. HMM represents persistence with transition probabilities. The Jump Model instead clusters observations and charges a penalty when the state changes. Shu et al. ask whether that simpler persistent state signal improves a 0/1 investment rule: hold the market in the favorable state and local cash in the unfavorable state.

The paper is therefore not “fit JM and compare clusters.” The economic test is whether the resulting strategy beats both buy-and-hold and an HMM strategy after delay and costs.

2. The whole experiment

  1. 1Daily data

    Equity log return for HMM; downside-risk features for JM.

  2. 2Online models

    Fit HMM from returns and JM from features, using past data only.

  3. 3State

    Label the current observation favorable or unfavorable.

  4. 4Position

    Favorable means 100% market; unfavorable means 100% cash. Signal at t earns t+2.

  5. 5Test

    Charge 10 bps per change and compare net Sharpe with HMM and buy-and-hold.

3. What did we change mathematically?

Fixed JM

A constant λ makes all state changes equally expensive. This reduces noisy switching but can react late.

Evidence-adaptive JM

Discount the switch cost when the destination state fits the data much better. Arrival evidence was responsive but noisy.

Lagged evidence

Use the previous observation's loss gap. This removes direct reuse of the current loss. It is the best tested JM variant in DE and JP, although buy-and-hold still wins in JP.

C_t(i,j) = λ₀ exp{-β tanh([L_{t-1}(i)-L_{t-1}(j)]₊ / q_train)}, i ≠ j

Pair-balanced: tilt the two directions oppositely while keeping C_t(i,j) + C_t(j,i) = 2λ₀, so the pair-average transition cost stays fixed.

q_train is a positive robust loss scale estimated from the 3,000-day training prefix only. Arrival and Lagged used β = 0, log(2), log(4); Balanced used 0 and log(4). β was scenario-fixed, not learned for profit, and β = 0 exactly equals fixed JM. On January/July refit dates, the prior loss is evaluated under parameters available at t, so the rule is causal at t but not strictly known at t−1. Formula, objective, toy paths, brute force, timing, and prefix invariance were verified.

5. What does this mean?

  • The checked implementation path is internally consistent: losses, penalties, states, signals, positions, trades, costs, and returns were checked end to end.
  • The mathematics moved: we built and verified a past-only family of time-varying JM transition costs.
  • The market objective is not met: no tested JM beats both benchmarks in all three markets. This is development evidence, not a profitability claim.

The next mathematical question should connect states more directly to future economic usefulness. A simple candidate is to fit state centers with both feature-clustering loss and a past-only t+2 return prediction loss. That would test whether regimes are not only compact, but also informative for the position we will take. It has not been run.

Protocol, limitations, and evidence links
  • HMM input: equity log return. JM features: DD-10, Sortino-20, Sortino-60.
  • Rolling model window: 3,000 observations. JM refits in January and July; HMM refits daily.
  • Monthly selection: preceding eight calendar years.
  • Frozen λ grid: 0, 5, 15, 35, 70, 150, 300, 600, 1200.
  • The evaluated proxy sample begins in 2007–2009, not the paper's exact 1990–2023 Bloomberg/GFD sample. Exact paper Sharpe is not the target.
  • The same through-2023 markets have been inspected repeatedly; no generalization or holdout claim is made. No reported model or P&L experiment used later rows, but a separate source audit inspected public candidate series through July 2026.
