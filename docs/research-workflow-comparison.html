Adaptive Jump Model Research Audit

What changed, what we tested, and what should happen next

A meeting brief for discussing the Adaptive Jump Model project after auditing the earlier P0/P1/P2 work, completing a proxy replication, and testing a longer JM training window.

Bottom line: the earlier positive-looking outputs were not comparable to the paper. The rebuilt pipeline is reproducible, but its free-source proxies begin too late for an exact replication. Fixed JM did not reproduce the paper's directional ranking on those proxies. A follow-up 4,000-day experiment then reached its preregistered lambda-grid boundary, so it correctly stopped before Sharpe was examined.

Meeting status in three observations

What has genuinely advanced

Data and protocol choices are frozen before results; signals are causal; runs carry hashes and checkpoints; a verifier independently reconstructs boundaries, accounting, metrics, and claims.

What has not happened

No adaptive-lambda experiment and no 2024-2026 model extension have run. The live monitor is code-complete but has not passed a canonical operational-acceptance run.

Decisions to ask the advisor: Should exact replication wait for the authors' series/code? Should the next frozen experiment expand the 4,000-day lambda grid? May adaptive and 2026 work proceed as a separately labelled exploratory proxy study if exact data remain unavailable?

Workflow 1: the archived legacy project

The old project contains two successive research directions. Both are preserved for provenance, but neither is active.

Legacy stage A - minute and tick experiments
InputKibot tick/minute files

IBM, OIH, IVE, and WDC local files; mixed adjusted OHLCV and bid/ask formats.

Question driftMicrostructure features

Spread, trade count, shocks, and noise scores. These are not the paper's daily return features.

ModelCustom HMM and JM stack

Hand-set duration-based penalty scale and a separate custom dynamic program.

EvaluationOne train/test split

No paper-style rolling 3,000-day fit plus eight-year monthly online validation.

OutputDemo backtests and reports

Useful engineering diagnostics, but not evidence about the Shu paper.

Legacy stage B - Claude's pre-audit daily P0/P1/P2 pivot
InputYahoo price data, cash return = 0

Required an uncommitted cache builder; no manifest, cutoff, or input hashes.

P0Approximate daily baseline

2,520-day fit, 3- or 5-year validation, 126-day blocks, clipping, and OOS from 2005.

Gate skippedBoundary-selected fixed penalty

The largest tested penalty was repeatedly selected, yet P1 started before the fixed baseline was settled.

P1Adaptive penalty experiment

A narrow exploratory null. It did nest fixed JM, but it was tested on an unsettled baseline.

P2 withdrawnAsymmetric switch costs

For two states, the proposed objective reduced to symmetric switching plus an endpoint term.

What was wrong

The research question, data, windows, risk-free return, decoder schedule, grid checks, and claim level differed from the paper. P2 also had a mathematical identifiability error.

What was still useful

The time-varying dynamic program was oracle-tested, train-only scaling and t+2 timing appeared in the daily pivot, and the archived Claude handoff explicitly withdrew P2.

Fair attribution: these statements come from archived code and the Claude-authored handoff. They describe verified properties of the implementation, not the agent's intent or general ability.

Workflow 2: the current verified v7 pipeline

The current system answers a narrower question: does fixed JM reproduce the paper's directional ordering on the best documented free proxy bundle?

1. AuditInspect free sources

Classify price versus total return, coverage, instrument, availability, and license.

2. FreezeHash research.toml v7

3. AcquireSix cutoff-safe series

4. PrepareCausal returns and features

Local cash availability, excess returns, DD-10, Sortino-20/60, and no clipping.

5. FitPaper-shaped fixed models

9. Accountt+2 return and 10 bps cost

10. VerifyRecompute from trade paths

Check identity locks, 131 immutable files, 18 boundaries, 27 metrics, and the claim.

Completed follow-up: test a longer JM training window

This experiment directly tested the proposed explanation that 3,000 observations might be too short. It changed only fixed JM's trailing window from 3,000 to 4,000 observations and retained the sealed v7 data, HMM control, costs, delays, metrics, and gates.

HypothesisMore history may stabilize JM

Compare JM-4,000 with the sealed JM-3,000 control, without changing the paper-shaped benchmark.

FreezeOne variable changed

The task, grid, primary estimand, tie rules, bootstrap, and stop conditions were fixed before the run.

Run46 minutes, three markets

The run produced 51 verified files and nine market-delay boundary rows.

GateUpper lambda selected

The 5% boundary rule failed in 8 of 9 rows, especially Germany at 31.51%-37.67%.

ResultPerformance remains sealed

Zero metric and bootstrap rows were exposed. A wider grid requires a new frozen experiment.

1 / 9 passedUS delay 1 selected the upper edge in only 2.05% of months.
8 / 9 failedOther rows reached 5.47%-37.67%, above the preregistered 5% limit.
No post-hoc SharpeThe gate prevented a favorable or unfavorable performance story after observing the boundary.
Scientific interpretation: longer training has been tested, but its performance effect is not yet identified. The observed result says the current lambda grid is inadequate for JM-4,000; it does not say that a longer window helps or hurts returns.

Workflow 3: the paper as documented

This is the strongest reconstruction supported by the paper. Amber text marks information that is not sufficiently disclosed for exact reproduction.

DataBloomberg total-return indices

S&P 500, DAX, and Nikkei 225 in local currency, 1970-2023. Exact tickers and fields unknown.

CashGFD three-month Treasury Bills

US, Germany, and Japan. Series IDs and daily conversion unknown.

FeaturesExcess-return features

Downside deviation with halflife 10 and Sortino ratios with halflives 20 and 60.

ModelsRolling HMM and fixed JM

Two states and 3,000-day windows; HMM daily, JM parameters every six months.

SelectionMonthly online cross-validation

Eight trailing years, strategy Sharpe objective. Complete grids and tie rules unknown.

InferenceOnline terminal regimes

Only information available by the decision date should determine the current state.

Allocation0/1 strategy

Bull state holds the index; bear state holds the local risk-free asset.

ExecutionOne-day delay and 10 bps

End-of-day signal t first earns return t+2; switching incurs one-way cost.

TestOOS 1990-2023

Three markets, plus robustness delays of 1, 5, and 10 trading days.

FindingJM leads directionally

Higher Sharpe than HMM/B&H and lower drawdown than B&H across all markets.

What could explain the current disagreement?

Scenario A: original data makes the result match

The main cause was data definition or the 1990 test window. The current code remains useful, and the proxy result should not be generalized.

Scenario B: original data still does not match

The next suspects are undisclosed grids, tie rules, HMM filtering, yield conversion, or another implementation convention.

Scenario C: author code and data reproduce their tables

We differential-test their artifact against ours one stage at a time: returns, features, states, choices, trades, then metrics.

Scenario D: changing our protocol creates a win

That is a new exploratory study, not a repair of this replication. The viewed 1990-2023 target cannot be tuned post hoc.

Scenario E: licensed data remain unavailable

The exact-reproduction question stays inconclusive. A transparent proxy study can continue, but its estimand and claims must remain separate.

Scenario F: fixed JM eventually passes the gate

Only then do we freeze and test adaptive lambda. The extension must nest fixed JM and use untouched post-audit evidence.

A concise explanation for an academic advisor

"At our previous meeting I showed preliminary P0/P1/P2 outputs. I later found that those experiments did not implement the paper's protocol closely enough, so I archived them and rebuilt the study around frozen, causal, auditable rules. The free-source proxy replication did not reproduce the paper's ranking. I then tested the longer-training-window hypothesis, but the lambda grid reached its preregistered boundary in eight of nine cases, so the protocol stopped before performance metrics were examined. I now need to decide whether to obtain the original data, expand that grid in a new experiment, or define adaptive and 2026 work as a separate exploratory proxy study."
Safe to say

The legacy claim was not comparable; v7 is internally verified; the proxy gate failed; longer training was attempted but reached a selection boundary; exact reproduction remains unresolved.

Not safe to say

The paper is wrong, longer training failed on performance, adaptive JM fails, the models are equivalent, or changing parameters after seeing results would repair replication.

Monitor status: present it only as tested engineering infrastructure. It has not yet completed a canonical model subprocess through cancel, restart, resume, verifier, and artifact-parity acceptance; Cloudflare deployment is also pending.
