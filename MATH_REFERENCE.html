Tick-Level Definitions

Mid Price

mid_t = (bid_t + ask_t) / 2

Center of the prevailing bid/ask quote at a trade timestamp.

Spread

spread_t = ask_t - bid_t

Quoted transaction friction. Crossed quotes are dropped and counted.

Relative Spread

rel_spread_t = spread_t / mid_t

Scale-free spread measure used for noise diagnostics.

Loaders fail loudly for schema, timestamp, non-numeric, and non-finite data. Market-quality rows such as crossed quotes are dropped and recorded in DataFrame.attrs["dropped_rows"].

Realized Variance

Realized variance follows the standard high-frequency definition: sum of squared log returns over a fixed interval. The return is assigned to the minute of the later tick.

r_i = log(P_i / P_{i-1}) RV_m = Σ r_i² for ticks i where floor(t_i, minute) = m
Overnight returns are excluded. If tick i and tick i-1 are on different calendar dates, r_i is not counted. This avoids treating overnight gaps as intraday realized variance.

Minute Returns And Volatility

Minute Mid Return

mid_return_m = log(mid_close_m / mid_close_{m-1})

Reset across dates. First minute per date is NaN.

Absolute Return

abs_mid_return_m = |mid_return_m|

Magnitude of the minute move, ignoring direction.

Log Volume

log_volume_m = log(1 + volume_m)

Compresses the scale of volume while keeping zero volume valid.

Rolling Volatility

rolling_vol_k = std(mid_return over last k rows)

Uses pandas sample standard deviation with full window required.

Z-Score Convention

All z-scores are computed over non-missing values.

z(x_t) = (x_t - mean(x)) / std(x)
  • Original NaN values remain NaN.
  • If valid standard deviation is unavailable or near zero, valid entries become 0.
  • This prevents a flat series from causing division by zero.

Raw Diagnostic Scores

Noise Score

noise_score_raw = z(rel_spread_close) - z(log_volume)

Higher when relative spreads are unusually wide and volume is unusually low. Interpreted as microstructure noise / low liquidity.

Shock Score

shock_score_raw = |z(mid_return)| + z(rolling_vol_20)

Higher when the minute return is unusually large or recent volatility is unusually elevated.

These are raw diagnostics, not calibrated probabilities. They should be inspected before using them in penalty functions.

How This Connects To The Adaptive Jump Model

The later jump model will minimize a path cost that looks conceptually like:

total_cost = fit_cost + Σ penalty_t(state_t != state_{t-1})

The adaptive part is that penalty_t changes with market conditions.

High Noise

noise_score_raw ↑ ⇒ penalty_t ↑

When data quality is noisy, make switching harder to avoid chasing quote noise.

High Shock

shock_score_raw ↑ ⇒ penalty_t ↓

When price movement looks abnormal, make switching easier to capture regime shifts.

Research Cross-Checks

  • Realized variance should be checked against realized volatility and quadratic variation literature.
  • Noise score should be checked against market microstructure noise and liquidity proxies.
  • Shock score should be checked against jump detection and volatility-regime literature.
  • Penalty mapping must preserve invariants: noise up means penalty up; shock up means penalty down.
