Part III · Features And Models

Walk-Forward Selection And Performance Measurement

How do we choose a smoothing setting using only the past, then compare complete strategies fairly?

Intermediate levelMonthly selection laboratoryPaper Sections 3.4.3 and 4
The opening question

Which rule was actually available before your $100 moved?

At a month-end, you have $100 and nine candidate JM penalties. Each candidate has produced historical online states using information that would have existed on each date. You turn those states into market-or-cash strategy returns with the frozen delay and transaction cost, score only the trailing eight years, and select one penalty for the next month.

Suppose the selected state requests cash. With a one-day trading delay, the request made after day t first controls the return at t+2. If the position changes and wealth is still $100, a 10-basis-point one-way cost removes $0.10, leaving $99.90 before that return. Selection, signal, execution, and earned return are four different events.

Causal monthly decision

Past → choice → future

Trailing validation selects one candidate. The choice is delayed and held forward until a later monthly decision replaces it.

A common failure: final-sample winner

Future → rewritten past

Choosing the candidate with best 1990-2023 performance and drawing it across 1990 makes every early decision depend on later returns.

A walk-forward study repeats the same permitted procedure at every decision date. It does not fit once, select once using the final table, or revise old choices. This makes the object under evaluation a complete algorithm: data definitions, features, fitting schedule, online inference, validation objective, tie rule, delay, cost, and metric definitions.

The idea to rememberFit parameters on trailing training data, generate candidate states as they would have appeared online, select smoothing on trailing validation strategy returns, apply the selected signal only after its delay, and measure all competitors on the same eligible dates. No later stage may reach backward.
Words before notation

Name every layer of the walk-forward clock

Model parameter

A quantity fitted from observations, such as a JM centroid or HMM transition probability.

Hyperparameter

A setting outside that fit, such as JM jump penalty λ or HMM filter length k, selected by validation.

Candidate surface

The audit table containing every decision date, candidate, valid-return count, validation score, and eligibility flag.

Out of sample

A period not used to make the parameter or hyperparameter decision being evaluated at that date.

Boundary gate

A pre-metric check that asks whether the upper search-grid value is selected too often, suggesting the grid may be too narrow.

Common sample

The identical complete date rows used for every competing strategy within one market and delay comparison.

A training window supplies data for fitted parameters. In the paper protocol it contains 3,000 observations. A validation window comes after enough training history and evaluates candidate complete strategies over the trailing eight calendar years. The outer testing path uses the candidate selected at each historical month-end.

Cadence means how often an operation occurs. The fixed JM estimates centroids initially and in January/July; its terminal state is inferred daily for every penalty. The HMM is fitted daily. Hyperparameter selection occurs monthly. Strategy returns occur on each eligible trading row. These clocks must not be collapsed into one vague "rolling model."

The Sharpe ratio compares average strategy excess return with strategy-return volatility. The project annualizes by multiplying the daily ratio by the square root of 252. Higher is preferred during monthly selection. A score is eligible only with enough paired valid strategy and cash returns, finite Sharpe, and a current candidate state.

A numerical tie rule resolves scores equal within a tiny fixed tolerance. The project chooses the lower numeric smoothing candidate: less JM penalty or shorter HMM filter. This prevents arbitrary column order or floating-point noise from deciding the strategy.

Grid boundary is the largest candidate. If it is selected frequently, an even larger untested value might have been preferred. The project's preregistered check counts upper-bound selections over OOS months. A fraction no greater than 5% passes. Metrics remain sealed until every model-delay boundary check passes.

Robustness delay repeats the complete validation and test accounting under an operational delay of 1, 5, or 10 trading days. It is invalid to select smoothing under one-day validation and merely shift the final strategy by ten days. Candidate selection must experience the same assumed delay as its test path.

Turnover is annualized average one-way position change. Leverage is average risky weight, which is the fraction of complete rows in position 1 for this binary strategy. Maximum drawdown is the worst percentage fall in cumulative wealth from its running peak. Expected shortfall averages returns at or below a chosen lower-tail quantile.

The complete workflow

Follow one month from raw history to sealed metrics

Fit and inferTrailing 3,000 observationsOne online state per candidate and date
ValidateTrailing eight calendar yearsSame delay, cash, and 10-bps cost
Select and tradeMonth-end choice, deterministic tieSignal applied after delay
Gate and measureUpper-bound frequency firstCommon-date OOS metrics second

First, create all candidate state histories without using outer outcomes for selection. JM columns correspond to fixed penalties; HMM columns correspond to causal filter lengths applied to daily terminal states. Convert state 0 to risky signal 1 and state 1 to cash signal 0.

Second, run each candidate signal through the accounting engine. A one-day delay shifts by two return rows. Gross return mixes equity and cash according to the delayed position. Every later position change pays 10 basis points; the frozen protocol does not charge the first valid allocation. The resulting net candidate returns, not raw state accuracy or in-sample objective, enter validation.

Third, on the last complete trading date of each month, score paired candidate and cash returns whose dates are later than the eight-year start boundary and no later than the decision date. Select the highest eligible Sharpe, with the lower-smoothing tie rule. Forward-fill that hyperparameter choice until the next decision and read the corresponding candidate state each day.

Fourth, before opening any OOS performance table, count how often each upper-grid value was selected in OOS months for every model and robustness delay. If any gate fails, the study stops; the grid cannot be expanded after inspecting favorable or unfavorable metrics. Only a passing study builds buy-and-hold, HMM, and fixed-JM paths on their intersection of complete metric rows.

Worked example two

Resolve a tie before the next month exists

Consider a toy JM grid [5, 35, 70]. Its month-end trailing validation Sharpes are 0.42, 0.58, and 0.58. The best score is tied. The frozen rule selects 35 because it is the smaller smoothing value. Column order cannot change the answer.

Now suppose 70 scores 0.59 while 35 remains 0.58. Candidate 70 wins. Because 70 is the toy grid's upper boundary, record an upper-bound selection. If this is the first such choice among 20 OOS months, the fraction is 1/20 = 5% and passes a no-greater-than-5% rule. If one earlier month also selected 70, the fraction becomes 2/20 = 10% and fails.

The boundary gate does not say candidate 70 is bad. It says the search may be truncated, so a scientific comparison is not ready to open. Expanding the grid and rerunning before metrics prevents the researcher from choosing a range in response to the performance table.

A common failure: score states instead of tradable paths

A candidate may match a retrospective regime chart yet trade poorly after latency, delay, and costs. The paper deliberately validates financial application performance using online inferred states. The project therefore creates delayed net returns for each candidate before computing Sharpe. This aligns the selection objective with the later strategy.

Another failure gives a model its own convenient sample. If HMM is missing difficult dates while JM is complete, separate row sets can make scores incomparable. The outer metric layer intersects complete required rows across buy-and-hold, HMM, and fixed JM for each market-delay comparison.

Connection to the named paper

Section 3.4.3 selects application performance online

Section 3.4.3 of Shu, Yu, and Mulvey (2024), arXiv:2402.05272v3 updates the optimal JM jump penalty monthly. For every candidate, it generates an eight-year sequence of online inferred regimes using the process from Section 3.4.2, applies the 0/1 strategy with one-day delay and a 10-basis-point one-way cost, and selects highest validation Sharpe for the following month.

The selected hyperparameter is itself delayed: a choice at the end of day t applies to day t+2 under the one-day convention. The paper uses the same framework to select HMM filter length. Starting data in 1970 supports about 12 years of training plus eight years of validation before the stated 1990-2023 testing period.

Connection to this project

The baseline study seals metrics behind diagnostics

src/adaptive_jump/walkforward.py builds fixed-JM candidate states, daily HMM terminal states, and HMM filter candidates. For each delay and model, it calls one monthly selector. The returned evidence includes selected signal, monthly choices, complete score surface, and every candidate's strategy returns.

The selector sorts numeric candidate columns, applies the accounting engine before scoring, waits eight calendar years after the first date on which all candidates are present, requires at least 252 paired valid returns, and uses only dates through each decision. Future changes are tested not to alter past choices or signals.

The frozen effective OOS start is computed per proxy market: it cannot precede the requested 1990 date and must follow the 3,000th complete feature row by eight calendar years. Shorter free-source coverage therefore shifts the comparison period rather than silently weakening history. That is one reason the study is labeled proxy replication.

src/adaptive_jump/backtest.py applies binary positions, delay, local cash, turnover, and cost. open_baseline_metrics refuses to run unless every boundary row passes. It then computes buy-and-hold, HMM, and fixed-JM metrics only on common complete rows for each delay.

tests/test_walkforward.py checks full validation history, ties, unsorted candidates, frozen delay/cost, future-prefix invariance, OOS-only boundary frequency, metric sealing, and common dates. tests/test_backtest.py checks offsets, costs, benchmark accounting, and metric formulas. The first equation uses mean strategy return minus mean cash return in the numerator, but strategy-return volatility in the denominator. Cash variability is not subtracted from the denominator. The same multiplier applies to all candidates at one decision, but using the exact frozen definition preserves parity with final metrics.

The second equation writes the tie rule: find best Sharpe, include candidates no more than tolerance below it, then choose the smallest numeric value. The candidate must also satisfy return-count, finite-score, and current-state eligibility checks.

CAGR compounds actual net returns. Annualized volatility is sample standard deviation times 252. Calmar uses annualized arithmetic mean excess return divided by absolute MDD in this project. Expected shortfall averages strategy returns at or below the 5% sample quantile. Turnover is mean one-way turnover times 252; leverage is mean binary risky position.

Evidence and limits

A rigorous backtest is evidence, not certainty

Verified mechanics

Tests establish trailing decisions, delay/cost parity, deterministic ties, boundary sealing, common dates, metric identities, and future-prefix invariance on controlled inputs.

Unresolved science

Proxy definitions, shifted market periods, finite-sample uncertainty, structural change, and future transportability still limit conclusions.

Evidence boundaryThe verified v7 report is the source for current through-2023 proxy metrics and conclusion. This chapter does not recalculate or soften that frozen result.
Selection limitationEight-year validation reduces but does not eliminate overfitting. One observed market history supplies overlapping monthly windows, candidate scores are noisy, and repeatedly redesigning the protocol after reading OOS outcomes consumes their confirmatory value.

Metrics answer different questions. Higher CAGR can accompany worse drawdown. Lower volatility can reflect lower leverage rather than superior timing. Sharpe can hide tail shape. Expected shortfall is sample-sensitive. Transaction costs are simplified. A scientific claim must state the primary endpoint in advance and report unfavorable, null, and favorable evidence consistently.

Retrieval practice

Audit the complete candidate, not just its score

Recall

Two eligible candidates have Sharpes tied within tolerance. Which one does the project select?

Choose an answer, then check the explanation.
Calculation: upper-bound gate

The upper candidate is selected in 3 of 80 OOS months. The fraction is 3.75%, which is no greater than 5%, so this diagnostic passes. Passing does not prove the grid or model is scientifically correct.

Error diagnosis: mismatched validation delay

Selecting under one-day delay and testing under ten-day delay evaluates a different candidate procedure. Recompute every candidate validation path with the ten-day assumption before selecting for that robustness run.

Error diagnosis: unequal metric dates

Differences may reflect missing periods rather than models. Intersect complete required rows across all compared strategies within each market and delay, then compute metrics.

Advisor explanation

"At each month-end we score each candidate's trailing eight-year online strategy with the same delay and cost used OOS, choose highest Sharpe with a lower-smoothing tie, hold that choice forward, require upper-grid diagnostics to pass, and compare B&H, HMM, and JM on common dates."

How to explain this to your advisor

Short version: "Our unit of comparison is the full causal procedure, not a model fit. Selection sees trailing net strategy returns only; final metrics remain sealed until search-boundary diagnostics pass."

Important qualification: "This protocol controls several major leakage and fairness risks. It cannot turn shifted free-source proxies into exact paper data or make one historical backtest definitive future evidence."
